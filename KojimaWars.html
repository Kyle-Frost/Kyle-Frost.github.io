The Descent

The Descent is a roguelike dungeon crawler developed for a 3-week Game Jam.

The game features a modular random dungeon generator that takes prefabs of precreated rooms to generate a maze of rooms, forming a dungeon. The system can automatically add or remove walls to ensure that a sure path from the start room to the boss room can be followed, whilst also preventing any open rooms or void space to be accessed.

The player is equipped with a bow and various elemental arrows to takedown enemies. Using the mouse aim and fire, the player has full control of their shots - able to adjust power to dynamically effect rate of fire and damage.

A tier-based loot system was added to give an uniqueness to each run, and allow the player to change up their playstyle based on the rewards they have recieved. These rewards drop as voxelized 3D objects, and give the player bonuses or special bonuses, with 3 types of loot spawning each with a rarity tier.

Consumables refill arrow ammunition or health.
Upgrades typically provide a boost to the player's stats, such as movement speed or bow power.
Relics provide special abilities or passives, such as reflecting a portion of damage back at the assailant, or gaining a small permanent light source.

The horror aspect is amplified with our torch system. The dungeon rooms you explore are lit only by torch flames, which slowly extinguish as you use them. Be too careless with your resources and you'll find yourself trying to fight off enemies with only the light of your flaming arrows to guide you.

Torches are randomly spawned inside rooms, and so players are encouraged to explore more to keep their resourced topped up. trying to manage your light whilst also utilising the spoils of loot is a good balance of horror and action, giving players various ways to approach the game
